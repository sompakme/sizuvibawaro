Early Life & Education

Amos Bocelli, now 28, was born in Italy on February 22, 1995. He is the oldest child of Andrea Bocelli and his first wife, Enrica Cenzatti, who married in 1992.

His father is a famous singer-songwriter and operatic tenor who has sold over 90 million records worldwide and has performed in many prestigious venues and events.

His mother is a former secretary who met his father while he was playing piano bars in Italy to support his law studies at the University of Pisa.

Amos grew up in the Tuscan resort of Forte dei Marmi, where his parents owned a villa. His parents divorced when he was six or seven years old, but they remained on good terms and raised their children together, living not far from each other.

Amos has a younger brother, Matteo, born on October 8, 1997, and a half-sister, Virginia, born on March 21, 2012, from his father’s second marriage to Veronica Berti. Amos followed in his father’s footsteps and ventured into the world of music pretty early in his life.

He started playing the piano at four and showed great talent and interest in music. He attended the Istituto Superiore di Studi Musicali L. Boccherini, where he obtained a Bachelor of Arts degree in piano. He also studied music theory, harmony, and composition.

Career

Amos Bocelli is a musician and pianist who has performed with his father several times.

He debuted as a pianist in 2013, when he accompanied his father on the piano for his live performance of ‘Love Me Tender‘ at the Teatro del Silenzio in Lajatico, Italy.

He also played the piano for his father’s duet with Jennifer Lopez, ‘Quizás, Quizás, Quizás,’ at the Dolby Theatre in Hollywood, California 2015.

Amos has been involved in producing his father’s latest album, ‘Sì’ (2018), his first album of new material in 14 years.

He is the pianist in the acoustic version of the songs ‘Sono Qui’ (I am Here) and ‘Ali di Libertà’ (Wings of Freedom), featured as bonus tracks in the album’s deluxe edition.

He also appeared in the music video for ‘Sono Qui,’ which shows him playing the piano in a studio with his father.

In addition to his musical career, Amos is also a member of the board of directors of his father’s business and charity organizations.

He joined the board of directors of Almud Edizioni Musicali, Bocelli’s business and management office, in January 2014.

He also became a part of the board of directors of ABF Andrea Bocelli Foundation, Bocelli’s charity initiative that supports education, health, and social projects in Italy and worldwide, in April 2016.
