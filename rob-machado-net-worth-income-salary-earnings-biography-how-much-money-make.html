He is best known by his surname Rob Machado. Born on October 16, 1973 in Sydney, Australia, Rob Machado is an Australian-born American professional surfer from Cardiff-by-the-Sea, a neighbourhood in the city of Encinitas (San Diego County).

Machado graduated from San Dieguito High School and is well-known for his “laid-back” and easygoing demeanour, which he exhibits both in and out of the water. Despite the fact that he surfs on a contemporary tri-fin shortboard, he is still referred to as a “soul surfer.” This is mainly due to his soft-spoken, modest demeanour and indifference in the limelight, as well as his fluid surfing technique, which has earned him the moniker “Mr. Smoothy.” He is one of the most well-known goofy-foot surfers in the world today, and he has a lot of fans. 

Rob has won a number of the most renowned competitions in professional surfing, including the Pipeline Masters (also known as the Triple Crown of Surfing) in Hawaii and the U.S. Open of Surfing, which is the biggest surfing tournament contested on the U.S. mainland. His appearances in surfing publications, as well as his movies and documentaries, help to keep him in the public eye. Each year, Rob hosts and takes part in a surf competition held at his home reef, the Rob Machado Surf Classic and Beach Fair.
