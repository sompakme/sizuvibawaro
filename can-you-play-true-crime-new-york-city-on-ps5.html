Bustin' Out the Past: Can You Play True Crime: New York City on PS5?

Ah, True Crime: New York City. Those were the days, partner. Precinct shootouts, busting bad guys (and sometimes good guys, depending on your karma), and rocking a sweet cop afro – all in the glorious concrete jungle. But here's the million-dollar question: can you relive those glory days on your spankin' new PS5?

The Verdict: Not Quite, But There's Hope (Maybe)

Hold onto your nightsticks, because True Crime: New York City is not officially playable on PlayStation 5. This is because the game originally came out in 2005 for the PS2, Xbox, and other bygone eras of gaming. So, for now, you can't suit up as Marcus Reed on your shiny new console.

But Don't Fret Yet, Detective!

Here's the good news: whispers on the internet streets hint at a possible remaster or even a sequel. There's even a fan-made trailer floating around that'll get your nostalgia juices flowing [YouTube True Crime New York City Remake Trailer PS5]. So, keep those fingers crossed, and who knows, you might be battling Triads in Chinatown on your PS5 one day!

In the Meantime, Here's How to Get Your True Crime Fix:

  • Backward Compatibility? Nope. PS5 isn't backwards compatible with PS2 games (unless you have a time machine – in which case, hit me up!).
  • Emulation Station: If you're a tech-savvy detective, emulation might be an option. But this can get a bit tricky, so proceed with caution (and maybe some online tutorials).
  • Alternative Games: Scratching that open-world, crime-busting itch? Check out games like Sleeping Dogs (spiritual successor to True Crime) or Grand Theft Auto for a dose of digital mayhem.
