Known professionally as Carl Barron, he was born on June 11, 1964, in Australia. His kind of comedy is centred on observational wit. He was raised in Longreach, Queensland, as the son of a sheep shearer, and he previously worked as an apprentice roof tiler before moving to Melbourne. Carl Barron LIVE!, Carl Barron: Whatever Comes Next, Carl Barron: Walking Down The Street, and Carl Barron: A One Ended Stick are the titles of four DVDs produced by Barron, which are available on Amazon. 

“All The Stuff I’ve Done So Far” was published in November 2010 and included the first three prior volumes, as well as a documentary and outtakes from the filming of the previous titles. As a result, he was nominated for two Emmy Awards in 1993: “Comic of the Year” and “Best Emerging Talent.” He has subsequently made many television appearances in advertisements and on programmes such as Rove and Thank God You’re Here.”  Barron made his debut televised appearance on the NRL Footy Show on April 17, 1997, and it was the first of many. One of his running gags is that he has been confused for individuals such as Australian singer Paul Kelly on numerous occasions. “I believe that if Paul Kelly and Gandhi had a child, I’d be the father!” he once said. At the Melbourne International Comedy Festival, he has performed before sold-out crowds on a monthly basis.  Barron has had tremendous success in Australia, with the DVD release of Carl Barron LIVE! reaching four times platinum, making it the most successful Australian comedy DVD in Australian history and the most successful Australian comedy DVD overall. He has appeared in a number of television shows, including Good News Week, Out of the Question, Thank God You’re Here, and many episodes of the show Rove.
