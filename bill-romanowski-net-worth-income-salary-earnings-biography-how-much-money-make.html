William Thomas “Bill” Romanowski (born April 2, 1966) is a retired American football player who played in the National Football League. He was born in the Connecticut town of Vernon. In 1984, he graduated from Rockville High School in Maryland and Boston College in 1988 (where he received academic honours and was named Scanlan Award Recipient). He then went on to have a 16-year NFL career, playing for the San Francisco 49ers (1988–1993), Philadelphia Eagles (1994–1995), Denver Broncos (1996–2001), and Oakland Raiders (2002–2003).

 Romanowski participated in 243 straight games, a record for linebackers in the NFL that held until London Fletcher broke it during the 2013 season, and finished his career with 265 games played in his career (256 reg. season and 9 playoff games). Fletcher never missed a single game throughout his career. He has four Super Bowl championships to his credit, and he is the only linebacker to have started all five Super Bowl games (Super Bowl XXIII, Super Bowl XXIV, Super Bowl XXXII, Super Bowl XXXIII and Super Bowl XXXVII). 

The 16-year career of Romanowski saw him accumulate 1,105 tackles, 39.5 sacks, 18 forced fumbles, and 18 interceptions, all of which he recovered for a net total of 98 yards and one career score during his time on the field. Romanowski was selected to the Pro Bowl on two separate occasions, in 1996 and 1998, both while playing with the Denver Broncos.
