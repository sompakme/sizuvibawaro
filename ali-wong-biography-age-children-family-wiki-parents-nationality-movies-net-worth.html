Early Life & Education

Ali Wong, now 41 years old, was born to Tam Wong and Adolphus Wong on April 19, 1982, in San Francisco, California.

Her mother is a Vietnamese American social worker who immigrated to the U.S. in 1960, and her father is a Chinese American anesthesiologist who worked for Kaiser Permanente for 30 years.

She is the youngest of four children and has an older brother, Andrew, an older sister, Mimi, and another sibling whose name is not publicly known.

She grew up in the Pacific Heights neighborhood of San Francisco and attended San Francisco University High School, where she was class president and editor of the school newspaper.

She graduated from UCLA in 2004, where she majored in Asian-American studies and was involved in the LCC Theatre Company, a comedy group that performed sketches and improv. She also studied abroad in Hanoi, Vietnam, where she learned Vietnamese.

Personal Life

Ali Wong met her husband, Justin Hakuta, at a wedding in 2010. Hakuta is a Harvard Business School graduate, Fulbright scholar, tech entrepreneur, and the son of famous TV inventor Ken Hakuta.

They married in 2014 and have two daughters, Mari and Nikki. Mari was born in November 2015, and Nikki was born in December 2017.

Both were featured in Wong’s Netflix comedy specials, Baby Cobra and Hard Knock Wife. Wong wrote a book dedicated to her daughters called Dear Girls: Intimate Tales, Untold Secrets & Advice for Living Your Best Life, which was published in 2019.

However, in 2022, Wong and Hakuta announced their divorce, citing irreconcilable differences. Wong has not revealed the details of their separation but has said they remain friends and co-parents.

Career

Ali Wong began her comedy career at 23, performing at open mic nights and small clubs in San Francisco.

She moved to New York in 2008, where she honed her skills and met other comedians, such as Amy Schumer, John Mulaney, and Aziz Ansari.

She also worked as a writer on Fresh Off the Boat, a sitcom based on the memoir of chef Eddie Huang.

She has appeared on several TV shows, such as Inside Amy Schumer, Black Box, and Are You There, Chelsea? She also voices characters on the animated series Tuca & Bertie and Big Mouth.

Wong rose to fame in 2016 when she released her first Netflix stand-up special, Baby Cobra, which she filmed while seven months pregnant with her first child.

The special was a huge success, earning rave reviews and millions of views. She followed it up with another Netflix special in 2018, Hard Knock Wife, which she filmed while pregnant with her second child.

The special was equally acclaimed and showcased Wong’s candid and hilarious take on motherhood, marriage, and feminism.

In 2020, she released her third Netflix special, Don Wong, a tribute to her father, who passed away in 2019. The special was well-received and demonstrated Wong’s ability to balance humor and emotion.

Wong has also ventured into acting, starring in the romantic comedy film Always Be My Maybe, which she co-wrote and co-produced with her longtime friend and fellow comedian Randall Park. The film, released on Netflix in 2019, was a hit and featured a cameo by Keanu Reeves.

Wong also starred in the Netflix dark comedy series Beef, which she co-created and co-executed with Lee Sung Jin. The series, which premiered in 2022, follows two people who get into a road rage incident and become obsessed with destroying each other’s lives.

Wong is widely regarded as one of her generation’s most original and influential comedians. She is known for her frank and fearless style of humor, which often tackles topics such as race, gender, sexuality, and motherhood.

She has faced challenges and stereotypes as an Asian American woman in the comedy industry but has never compromised her voice or vision.

She has been praised by critics and fans alike. She has received several awards and nominations, including the American Comedy Award for Best Comedy Special, the Critics’ Choice Television Award for Best Actress in a Comedy Series, and the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing for a Variety Special.
