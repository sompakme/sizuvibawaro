Johnny Lee Harra, born Harry Lee Lovett, (July 11, 1946 – March 30, 2011) was an American Elvis impersonator who was most known for his rendition of Elvis Presley. When he was 11 years old, he started imitating Elvis, and in 1981, he was cast as Elvis, who was 42 at the time of the film This Is Elvis. He appeared in two sequences from the re-enactment of Elvis’ death on August 16, 1977, which was shot at Graceland and starred as one of four Elvis impersonators in the film. He returned to the stage just in time for the 25th anniversary of Elvis’ death, after spending many years in isolation. In the year 1995, the country was ranked second in the world as the greatest imitator of the twentieth century. The title was stolen by a Colombian, and Harra was featured in newspapers and publications, as well as on national television programmes such as Merv Griffin, Johnny Carson, and Dick Clark, to name a few examples. Johnny toured the world with his “Profiles of Presley” performance, which was a hit with audiences. He passed away on March 30, 2011, at the age of 64.
