  • Singer BNXN has advised upcoming artists on how to make money without depending on record labels
  • In an interview with Ascent Tunes, he noted that most upcoming artists believe they cannot make it without a record label
  • He added that they should ensure their songs are good enough for people to stream and listed how much they would make on different platforms

Daniel Benson, Nigerian singer professionally known as BNXN, formerly Buju, has generated reactions with the kind of advice he gave to his colleagues.

In an interview with Ascent Tunes, the singer, who fell off-stage last year, said artists should stop thinking they cannot make it without a record label.

The music star said most upcoming artistes rely too much on record labels. He explained that they should stream their songs on major platforms and listed the benefits they would gain from doing so.

BNXN lists what he makes

Listing the amount of dollars he makes on each platform, he said if he has one million streams on Apple, he gets $7,500, while on Spotify, he gets $5,500.

The music act, who shot his song in Jerusalem, added that money was needed for ideas to flourish. He encouraged artists to look for money to make up for the idea they have in their heads.

Giving more insights, he noted that he wished there was an artist bank where talents can present their ideas, and they would gauge it to know what it was worth and give them loans to fast track their ideas.
