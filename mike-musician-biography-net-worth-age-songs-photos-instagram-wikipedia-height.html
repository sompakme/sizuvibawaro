Early Life &  Education

Michael Jordan Bonema, now 25 years old, was born on October 13, 1998, in Livingston, New Jersey. He grew up in a multicultural environment, as his family moved frequently between Livingston, London, Essex, Philadelphia, and New York City.

Mike’s parents divorced when he was young, and he had a close relationship with his mother and father. He also has sisters who played a significant role in his life.

At 5, Mike’s mother moved him to England, seeking better educational opportunities. They lived in Hackney, London, for five years, where Mike experienced a different culture.

At age 10, he became interested in making music after being exposed to English Grime artist Skepta.

When he turned 12, Mike returned to Philadelphia with his father, immersing himself in American hip-hop culture.

Eventually, they settled in The Bronx, and Mike later moved to Brooklyn, where he co-founded the underground hip-hop collective called Slums.

Career

Mike’s interest in hip-hop began to develop while living in England in 2008. He was exposed to artists like Skepta, Chipmunk, and N-Dubz, as well as contemporary R&B artists such as Chingy and Bow Wow.

Upon returning to Philadelphia in 2010, Mike delved deeper into the American hip-hop scene, studying the works of artists like Chance the Rapper, Drake, and Lil Wayne.

In 2012, Mike recorded his first-ever song, a cover of MF Doom and Madlib’s “All Caps.” As he grew older, he developed his taste in hip-hop, drawing inspiration from artists like Tyler, the Creator and Earl Sweatshirt.

Mike began releasing music independently in 2015, gaining attention from listeners and industry professionals.

In 2017, Mike experienced a breakthrough with his full-length project, “May God Bless Your Hustle.” The mixtape garnered critical acclaim and established him as a rising star in the hip-hop scene.

He went on to release several follow-up projects, including “Black Soap” in 2018, “Renaissance Man” in 2018, and “War in My Pen” in 2018.
