23 Barbecue Sides You Can Make in the Slow Cooker

September 02, 2024
blog

Come summer, we’re all for cooking as much food on the grill as possible. We love the smoky, charred taste of grilled food and the way it cooks just a little bit unevenly — vegetables that are blackened at the edges but still al dente inside and meat with a charred crust and an almost-rare center. Plus, grilling outside keeps you from having to turn on the oven and add more heat to your already hot house.

The thing is, a grill can only fit so much food at once, so while it’s great for cooking up a main dish, there isn’t always room for sides. Plenty of summer side dishes don’t need to be cooked at all — think juicy tomato and burrata salads or crispy coleslaw with just enough mayo — but for those that do, we prefer to use a slow cooker.

If you think “summer slow cooker” is an oxymoron, we don’t blame you. One of the slow cooker’s best and most famous tricks is that it can turn inexpensive, tough cuts of meat into tender, pull-apart piles of wintry stews and braises. The results are pretty damn impressive, but not exactly the kind of thing you crave in warmer summer months.

The thing is: The slow cooker can do pretty much everything your oven does (except turn your kitchen into a midsummer sauna). Plus, the set-it-and-forget-it nature of the slow cooker means that it’s great for preparing party side dishes ahead of time and not having to think about them until it’s time to eat. You can even plug your slow cooker in to an outdoor electrical outlet if you really don’t feel like adding heat to your house!

Whether you’re cooking for two minutes or 20, here are the summer slow cooker bbq side dishes you need to know about.

  • Slow Cooker Black-Eyed Peas

    The smoky, savory flavor of these black-eyed peas slow cooked with a ham hock might just steal the thunder from your barbecue entrees.

    Get the recipe at Dinner at the Zoo.

  • Slow Cooker Mashed Potatoes

    When you’re grilling outside to keep the house cool, the last thing you want to do is put a pot of water on the stovetop to boil potatoes. Switch to a slow cooker mashed potatoes recipe instead, and you can have your favorite side dish without turning the kitchen into a steam room.

    Get the recipe at Lil’ Luna.

  • Slow Cooker Bacon Cheddar Corn Spoon Bread

    This soft, buttery, corn-filled spoon bread is even better when bacon and cheddar are added, and don’t be afraid to experiment with adding your own favorite spices and herbs.

    Get the recipe at Southern Bite.

  • Slow Cooker Sweet Potatoes

    The earthy, honey-like taste of these slow cooker sweet potatoes pairs beautifully with barbecue sauce. Grab your grilled favorites and pile them on your plate alongside a perfectly tender slow cooked sweet potato.

    Get the recipe at Well Plated by Erin.

  • Slow Cooker Creamed Corn

    If you’re used to creamed corn from a can, get ready to have your mind blown by this slow cooker creamed corn recipe. The corn actually tastes fresh and sweet, and the cream cheese-based sauce, along with the bacon and green onion garnish, is totally decadent.

    Get the recipe at Cooking Classy.

  • Slow Cooker Funeral Potatoes

    Also sometimes called Mormon funeral potatoes, this cozy comfort food recipe stars frozen potatoes O’Brien with a sauce made with cream cheese, sour cream, cream-of-soup, and plenty of cheddar cheese, too. The end result? A family-friendly hit.

    Get the recipe at Show Me the Yummy.

  • Buffalo Chicken Tater Tot Casserole

    What pairs well with Buffalo chicken tater tot casserole? The better question is: What doesn’t?

    Get the recipe at Slow Cooker Gourmet.

  • Slow Cooker Bacon Cauliflower

    Cauliflower is the trendiest vegetable around, and this slow cooker bacon and cauli dish is the perfect way to include it in any summer spread.

    Get the recipe at 365 Days of Slow + Pressure Cooking.

  • Slow Cooker Creamy Polenta

    Polenta is most often served with braised meat, but if you’re slow cooking a big cut of beef or pork on the grill, you’ll end up with a similar kind of super-tender meat that works really well with this creamy side.

    Get the recipe at Just a Little Bit of Bacon.

  • Garlic-Parmesan Potatoes

    Meat and potatoes make a great pair, but that doesn’t mean you can’t cook one on the grill and the other in your slow cooker.

    Get the recipe at The Magical Slow Cooker.

  • Slow Cooker Corn & Jalapeño Dip

    It isn’t a party without dip, and this sweet-spicy version is great for both scooping up with chips as an appetizer and later alongside a perfectly charred steak.

    Get the recipe at Damn Delicious.

  • Slow Cooker Baked Potatoes

    Whether you serve the baked potatoes as is or dice them up for potato salad, these no-fuss spuds come out perfectly every time.

    Get the recipe at Cooking Classy.

  • Slow Cooker Bourbon Baked Beans

    Can you heat canned baked beans to serve alongside your burger spread? Sure. Will those canned beans be laced with booze and bacon? Nope.

    Get the recipe at Plain Chicken.

  • Slow Cooker Macaroni & Cheese

    Ever tried a hot dog topped with mac and cheese? If not, you should definitely add it to your summer to-do list.

    Get the recipe at The Cookie Rookie.

  • Slow Cooker Barbecue Green Beans

    Far more delicious than the green bean dish you might eat on Thanksgiving (in our opinion, anyway), this one is studded with bacon and sauced with a tangy mixture of ketchup and barbecue sauce.

    Get the recipe at Spicy Southern Kitchen.

  • Slow Cooker Corn on the Cob

    Full disclosure: With this recipe, you won’t get intermittently charred kernels like you do when you cook corn on the grill, which is a bummer. That said, corn for a crowd takes up a whole lot of grill real estate that might be better used for cooking up burgers or dogs, so this set-it-and-forget-it alternative is great for a party. Plus, the cobs cook in a bath of melted butter, so you know they’ll be delicious.

    Get the recipe at Cooking Maniac.

  • Easy Slow Cooker Cornbread

    No summer barbecue is complete without a big helping of cornbread. And sure, it’s pretty easy to bake your batter in a skillet, but it’s even easier to pour that batter right into a slow cooker and let it do its thing. You won’t have to worry about burning the edges or the top either since the slow cooker method means that everything will cook more evenly.

    Get the recipe at Fuss Free Flavours.

  • Bacon Ranch Chicken Dip

    This recipe calls for an Instant Pot, but if you don’t have one, a slow cooker will work just fine! You’ll just need to complete the first few steps in a pan and then do the rest in your slow cooker. This delicious dip also makes a great appetizer!

    Get the recipe at The Skinny Pig.

  • Slow Cooker Baby Carrots with Honey and Brown Sugar

    Crowd-pleasing glazed baby carrots are even better when made in the slow cooker, so you can focus on the grill. The brown sugar honey glaze pairs well with barbecued items, and makes the carrots tasty enough that some people might actually want to put a vegetable on their plate of ‘cue.

    Get the recipe at Home Cooking Memories.

  • Slow Cooker Garlic Parmesan Spaghetti Squash

    We can think of about a million ways to serve this slow cooker garlic parmesan spaghetti squash this summer! Dress it with pesto made from fresh garden herbs, serve it with a cherry tomato bruschetta topping, add grilled chicken or shrimp — it’s so tasty, folks might not even realize they’re eating squash and not pasta.

    Get the recipe at My Modern Cookery.

  • Slow Cooker Basil Pesto Bread

    Cooking up homemade bread is even easier in the slow cooker, where it rises and bakes at the same time. This basil pesto bread would be delicious with grilled veggies, summer salads, or to soak up all the saucy goodness from your plate of barbecue.

    Get the recipe at Diethood.

  • Slow Cooker Cheesy Bacon Ranch Potatoes

    Making a dish like this on the stove top or in the oven will turn your home into an inferno in the summer. Try making your cheesy bacon ranch potatoes in the slow cooker instead, and keep your kitchen comfy.

    Get the recipe at Slow Cooker Meals.

  • Slow Cooker Broccoli Casserole

    This simple but delicious broccoli casserole can be made in the slow cooker in less than two hours. It doesn’t use any suspect condensed soups, and the garnish of fried onions adds a craveable crunch before serving.

    Get the recipe at Persnickety Plates.
