Darvin Ham reacts to the longtime feud between LeBron James and Ime Udoka

August 07, 2024
blog

Los Angeles Lakers head coach Darvin Ham had some strong words to say when reacting to the recent beef between LeBron James and Houston Rockets HC Ime Udoka, which actually dates back quite some time, according to Ramona Shelbourne of ESPN.

Udoka, 46, called James a "soft a-- boy" after the Lakers player asked him to stop calling them "b----es." The Rockets head coach was ejected for receiving his second technical foul.

James, 38, and Udoka brushed off the incident during their respective postgame press conference, but Shelbourne reported that Ham is not letting this go just yet.

"I have not heard [Darvin Ham] respond to a situation like this, since he was a player," Shelburne said. "When he was on the Pistons, on the bad boy Pistons, with the enforcer mentality. The language that Ime Udoka seemed to have used towards LeBron James included the b-word. That is not something you say to James and he said something back to him.

"Darvin Ham essentially said to me, I have it censored here on television here, 'There ain't no crying b----es over here, Saginaw said so.' Ham is from Saginaw, James is his best player, you come at his best player like that, there is a reaction. ... There's going to be things said between these two teams for years to come."

How did LeBron James' beef with Ime Udoka start?

James played against Udoka eight times back in the early 2000s and constantly targeted him on the floor. The longtime feud seemingly started during those matchups and now has a new chapter.

Shelbourne believes the Lakers and Rockets are now going to become a rivalry, especially considering James also dislikes Dillon Brooks.

James now has a few rivalries in the league, including with Brooks and Udoka, plus the Detroit Pistons big man Isaiah Stewart.

Udoka is in his first season as the Rockets head coach after leaving the Boston Celtics amid a cheating scandal with a franchise employee on ex-fiancée Nia Long.
