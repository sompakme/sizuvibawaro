What is the history of Fresno?

Fresno’s history dates back to its founding by the Central Pacific Railroad Company in 1872. Leland J. Stanford, a Director for the Railroad, selected the site for the city. During a scouting party in 1871, Stanford noticed a lush and green wheat field owned by A.Y. Easterby in the midst of the prairie’s dryness. Fresno has since grown and developed, becoming a vibrant city with a strong agricultural heritage.

What is Fresno California named after?

Fresno, California is named after the Spanish word for “ash tree.” The city received its name due to the abundance of mountain ash or ash trees found in the county. “Fresno” signifies the presence of these trees, which were prominent in the area during its early settlement.
